Discovering a historic city

Istanbul

Istanbul is home to some of Turkey's most historical and captivating sights. Explore crumbling Roman ruins and marvel at majestic Ottoman mosques, play with friendly street cats or sample tasty Turkish sweets - Istanbul has something for everyone.

From Mosques to Meows

Istanbul's streets are full of friendly cats and dogs. In many parts of the city, locals feed and take care of the street animals, by setting up housing and keeping the animals topped up with food and water. The cats and dogs repay them by acting all friendly and welcoming.

Don't be nervous about visiting Istanbul's stunning mosques. The Blue Mosque in particular is unmissable. Just keep in mind a few rules and you're sure to have a worthwhile experience.

Mosque Cultural Tips:

  • Women should cover their hair with a scarf or hat
  • Both men and women should wear long sleeved tops and trousers
  • Shoes must be removed before entering a mosque

Eat Your Heart Out in Istanbul

Another delight of Istanbul is its thrilling variety of delicious dishes. Meat-lovers won't be disappointed. There are juicy kebabs available everywhere you turn.

Turkish baking is unmissable: try pide (otherwise known as Turkish pizza), borek (fluffy layers of pastry festooned with cheese and spinach, or simit (Turkish bagel with sesame).

Other Turkish delicacies to look out for:

  1. Lokum, better known as Turkish delight
  2. Pistachio baklava
  3. Salep, a hot drink made from lotus root and topped with cinnamon
  4. Ayran, a Turkish sour yoghurt drink
